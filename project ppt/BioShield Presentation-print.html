Motivation

Why Biomedical Deepfake Text Matters

⚠️
Clinical Risk

LLM-generated fake PubMed abstracts are indistinguishable to human readers. Clinicians may act on fabricated findings.

⚡
Adversarial Arms Race

A static detector trained once is trivially defeated by a generator optimized to evade it. Robustness requires an adversarial loop.

🔬
Open Research Gap

Prior work targets general-domain detection. Biomedical text has unique structure — specialized detectors and evaluations are needed.

Central Question: Does the choice of fake generator family dominate detector robustness — more than adversarial retraining rounds or agent-guided rewriting?

BioMistral-7B — Fake Text Generator

Used in Conditions A (zero-shot baseline), C (+ Qwen rewrites), D (+ rewrites + retrain).

Base ArchitectureMistral-7B continued pretraining on PubMed Central. Causal LM, 7B parameters.
Why BioMistralDomain-adapted to biomedical literature — produces abstracts stylistically identical to real PubMed text. Same distribution as detector training data.
Fine-tuning ObjectiveCausal LM — next-token prediction on real PubMed abstracts. labels = input_ids; padding masked with -100.
Loss FunctionCross-Entropy over vocabulary logits. Minimizes negative log-likelihood of the next token at each position.

SeqGAN — Out-of-Family Baseline

Used exclusively in Condition B — tests detector generalization to a non-LLM generator family.

ArchitectureWord-level LSTM generator. Embedding → 1-layer LSTM → linear head over vocabulary.
VocabularyTop-k tokens from PubMed corpus. Specials: <pad> <bos> <eos> <unk>.
Training MethodMLE pretraining (teacher forcing). GAN REINFORCE step intentionally omitted — Cond. B needs a weak, runnable baseline generator.
Loss FunctionCrossEntropyLoss — next-token prediction. Padding ignored via ignore_index=vocab[<pad>].
Architecture Params
Embedding dim32
Hidden dim256
Seq length128 tokens
OptimizerAdam · lr = 1e-3
Key Result · Condition B

Evasion flat at ~99% across all 3 rounds of retrain. SeqGAN's LSTM token distribution is too far from biomedical English — detector boundary never covers SeqGAN's output manifold.

Evaluation · Metrics

Metrics — What, Why, and How Each Was Used

AUC ROC Area Under Curve

What: probability that the detector ranks a random fake above a random real.


0.5 = random  ·  1.0 = perfect

Why: threshold-free. Robust to class imbalance. Gold standard in medical AI.

F1 Harmonic Mean P&R

What: harmonic mean of precision and recall over the fake class.


F1 = 2 · (P · R) / (P + R)

Why: penalizes both missed fakes and false alarms. 50/50 balance makes F1 directly comparable across conditions.

ER Evasion Rate — headline

What: fraction of fakes labeled as real. Computed on each round's fresh 250-sample fake pool.


ER = FP / (FP + TN) at threshold 0.5

Why: adversary-aligned — measures how often a fake slips through in a deployed system. Per-round arms-race signal.

Key Contrast

Generator family explains a 100× spread in evasion (SeqGAN 99% vs BioMistral 0.7%). Adversarial retraining and agent rewrites had zero measurable effect when generator is in-family.

⚠ C = D Pathology

Conditions C and D produce byte-identical metrics. Baseline hits val-AUC 0.999 — the ceiling. Best-val-AUC rolls back to round 0 weights; no retrained checkpoint ever fires.

Key Finding

Generator-Family Dependence Dominates

The choice of generator family is the single most important factor — larger than adversarial retraining, agent rewriting, or scale.

Conclusions & Future Work

What We Built, What We Learned

Three Defensible Claims
Claim 1

Biomedical detectors are generator-family bound. SeqGAN 99% vs BioMistral 0.7% — 100× spread at 3k scale.

Claim 2

Best-val-AUC checkpoint hides the retraining signal when the generator is too easy. C = D is an evaluation design lesson, not a pipeline bug.

Claim 3

Cross-domain transfer is open. RAID 98% evasion motivates cross-domain training — future work, not a weakness.

Next Steps to Publish
  • Harder generator — LoRA-fine-tune BioMistral to evade the detector via DPO / reward-weighted fine-tuning. Directly addresses the C=D pathology.
  • Better checkpoint criterion — replace best-val-AUC with best-evasion-on-held-out-pool to make adversarial retraining adversarially aware.
  • Transfer attack — populate transfer_test.csv with Llama-3.2-3B fakes. Second cross-family data point completes the generator matrix.
